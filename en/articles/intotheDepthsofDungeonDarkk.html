>The company for which I had made DDD came back to tell me they wanted a 3D game. So I took this opportunity to redo the entirety of DDD's code, to fix the flaws my testers had highlighted.
First off, the tutorial is now a picture, which is both faster and clearer than the levels in the previous version. Then you go straight into the procedural levels, which were the most interesting part of the game. You now move by dragging your finger rather than pressing it, which feels more natural to most people. All skeletons play at the same time rather than one after the other, which greatly improves the game's flow. Finally, how much of a level you see at once has increased, thanks to a new camera perspective.
