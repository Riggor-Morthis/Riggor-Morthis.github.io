A week-long game jam, about creating a game resembling those included in the Nokia 3310s. Therefore, it was a jam all about constraints: 1-bit colour palette, only one sound channel, 10 inputs tops, 48x84 resolution... I really like this kind of project, as I believe obstacles breed creativity, which is why I really enjoyed this jam.
This jam's theme was "Fast&Slow", which reminds me of rally races, where sinuous hairpins are interspersed with straight lines where you have to go pedal-to-the-metal. Wanting to offer a lot of content despite the short dev time, I used procedural generation for my races' layout; more specifically, I used wave function collapse, because I've wanted to try it for a long time but the opportunity had not yet presented itself.
Unfortunately, I spent more time experimenting and learning than expected, which was at the expense of a smoother vehicle rotation, despite it being planned. I still don't regret learning a new proc-gen method, and thinking outside of my box.
