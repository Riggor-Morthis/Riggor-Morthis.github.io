I took part in this jam because of both my passion for TTRPGs and my passion for technical constraints. The goal was to create a TTRPG that would fit on a single sheet of paper. Furthermore, I also tried to question our relationship with randomness in TTRPGs.
In "A Grand Card Heist", players are dealt a hand like in a card game, and are then free to bluff about what they have to try and be the only burglar left to get the loot. Although this game incorporates a notion of randomness, it gives the players a lot of control as it allows them to distribute their "resources" as they please, as well as being in a competition against one another.
In "The Witches' Brew", players don't roll their dice, but must instead flick it towards a central piece, as in a game of "pétanque". Then, the distance between this piece and their dice dictates their degree of success. So, this RPG doesn't use randomness at all, but instead the players rely on their own dice-throwing skill.
Finally, "Band Of Critters" resembles a classic TTRPG, and my aim was to fit as much information as possible into this single sheet of paper. This way, a table can play out an entire campaign based solely on rules that can be read in 5 minutes: the very essence of this jam, which I've tried to push to the limit.
