An university project for our augmented reality course, our goal was to make a "board game" on Unity, designed for mobile and using AR features during gameplay. I ended up being the only one working on this project, which explains its lack of features.
On the video, you can see playing cards with QR codes on them (to differentiate them), onto which cubes (which should have been rooms) are displayed. The application will link these rooms together with beautiful paths, made thanks to some Bézier curves, connecting the closest neighbours together. This way, we can dynamically change the room layout of our board, and therefore the order in which they would have been explored. Once the player is satisfied with the board he built, he can save it by pressing a button, and the board is memorised in the most "central" card, so that the other cards can be reused later on during a game.
